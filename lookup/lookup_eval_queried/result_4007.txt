Studies with bath-applied cholinergic agonists have shown suppression of the fast component of the inhibitory postsynaptic potentials (IPSPs) evoked by stimulation of the association fibers. It was previously demonstrated that an associative form of LTP can be induced by coactivation of the two fiber systems after blockade of the fast, \u03b3-aminobutyric acid-A\u2013mediated IPSP. In this report, we demonstrate that an associative form of long-term potentiation can be induced by coactivation of afferent and intrinsic fibers in the presence of the cholinergic agonist carbachol.
