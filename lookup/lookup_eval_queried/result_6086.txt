Most patients with acquired HLH have no known underlying immune deficiency. Both acquired and genetic forms are triggered by infections, mostly viruses, or other stimuli. HLH also occurs as a complication of rheumatic diseases (macrophage activation syndrome) and of malignancies. The recent discovery of several genetic defects causing FHLH as well as the identification of the genes responsible for CHS, GS and XLP have underscored the role of granule (perforin/granzymes)-mediated cytotoxicity in both the killing of infected cells and the termination of the immune response. The immediate aim of therapy is suppression of the increased inflammatory response by immunosuppressive/immunomodulatory agents and cytotoxic drugs.
