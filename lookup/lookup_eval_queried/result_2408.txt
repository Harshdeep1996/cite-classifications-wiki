"<p>Caryosyntrips appendages have previously been reported from the Burgess Shale (Cambrian, Stage 5), British Columbia, Canada. New specimens of the genus are here reported from the Wheeler Formation (Cambrian, Drumian) and Langston Formation, Spence Shale Member (Cambrian, Stage 5), Utah, USA. The original Burgess Shale specimens are re-examined alongside the new specimens. Caryosyntrips is shown to have paired ventral spines on each podomere. Three species of Caryosyntrips are recognised: C. serratus Daley and Budd, C. camurus nov. sp. and C. durus nov. sp., differentiated by the overall shape of their appendages and arrangement of dorsal and ventral spines. These differences have potential implications for the feeding methods employed by different species of Caryosyntrips. This identification is supported by the overall shape of the fossil, and the presence, orientation, and height:width ratio, of ventral spines. However the dorsal surface of the appendage is not well preserved, and the appendage and its ventral spines are larger than other known Caryosyntrips. Therefore it is left in open nomenclature as C. cf. camurus.
