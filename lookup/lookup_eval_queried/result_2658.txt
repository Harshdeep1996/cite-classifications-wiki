Modern tree sloths are represented by two genera,<jats:italic>Bradypus</jats:italic>and<jats:italic>Choloepus</jats:italic>(both around 4\u20136 kg), whereas the fossil record is very diverse, with approximately 90 genera ranging in age from the Oligocene to the early Holocene. Modern Vermilingua are represented by three genera,<jats:italic>Cyclopes</jats:italic>,<jats:italic>Tamandua</jats:italic>and<jats:italic>Myrmecophaga</jats:italic>, with a size range from 0.25 kg to about 30 kg, and their fossil record is scarce and fragmentary. Statistics were significantly different from the null-hypothesis, supporting the hypothesis that body size variation correlates with the phylogenetic pattern. Most of the correlation is concentrated within Vermilingua, and less within Mylodontidae, Megatheriidae, Nothrotheriidae and Megalonychidae. Influence of basal metabolic rate (BMR), dietary habits and substrate preference is discussed. In anteaters, specialised insectivory is proposed as the primary constraint on body size evolution. In the case of sloths, mylodontids, megatheriids and nothrotheriids show increasing body size through time; whereas megalonychids retain a wider diversity of sizes.
